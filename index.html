Dental Disorder Patients in Indonesia

CONCLUSION

Based on the data beside, over the past five years, the Indonesian population has experienced an increase of one million people with dental disorders. This indicates that education regarding dental care and its implementation is still lacking. Additionally, the cost of visiting a dentist is quite high for the average per capita income of the Indonesian people.

Dental Health Counseling in Indonesia

Educational Campaigns

These are often conducted in schools, communities, and health centers, focusing on teaching proper brushing techniques, the importance of fluoride, and the prevention of cavities and gum diseases.

Collaborations with Healthcare Providers

Dental professionals work together with schools and community health centers (Puskesmas) to offer regular check-ups and treatments. These initiatives also include awareness programs on the connection between dental health and overall well-being.

School Dental Health Program (UKGS)

This program targets school-aged children to ensure they receive early dental care education. It includes routine dental check-ups and treatments, such as fluoride application and the distribution of toothbrushes.

Maternal and Child Health Programs

These initiatives aim to educate pregnant women and parents on the importance of early childhood dental care, promoting healthy habits from infancy.

Rural Outreach Programs

In more remote areas, mobile clinics and volunteer programs provide dental services to underserved communities, ensuring access to care and education.
