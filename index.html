Dental Disorder Patients in Indonesia

CONCLUSION

Based on the data beside, over the past five years, the Indonesian population has experienced an increase of one million people with dental disorders. This indicates that education regarding dental care and its implementation is still lacking. Additionally, the cost of visiting a dentist is quite high for the average per capita income of the Indonesian people.

Dental Check-up

It is crucial to schedule regular dental check-ups to ensure your oral health is maintained. Dentists can identify potential issues before they become serious. Aim for a check-up every six months for optimal care.

Oral Hygiene

Good oral hygiene practices include brushing your teeth twice a day, flossing daily, and rinsing with mouthwash. These steps can help prevent cavities and gum disease.

Healthy Diet

A healthy diet plays a significant role in maintaining oral health. Foods rich in calcium, phosphorus, and vitamins are essential. Limit sugary snacks and drinks to reduce the risk of cavities.
