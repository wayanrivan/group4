Explanation of the Most Common Dental Problems Suffered

Dental Caries (Tooth Decay)

This is the most prevalent dental problem in Indonesia. It occurs due to the buildup of plaque and bacteria, leading to cavities. Poor oral hygiene, a diet high in sugar, and limited access to dental care in rural areas contribute to its high incidence.

Periodontal Disease (Gum Disease)

Many Indonesians suffer from gum diseases, including gingivitis and periodontitis. These conditions are often caused by inadequate brushing and flossing, resulting in the buildup of plaque along the gum line, eventually leading to inflammation, bleeding, and in severe cases, tooth loss.

Tooth Loss

Due to a lack of timely treatment for caries and gum disease, many people experience tooth loss, especially among the elderly. Limited access to dental care and the high cost of treatment contribute to this issue.

Malocclusion (Misaligned Teeth)

Malocclusion is also common, especially in younger populations. It refers to misaligned teeth or improper jaw positioning, which can lead to difficulty in chewing, speech problems, and self-esteem issues.

Oral Cancer

While less common than caries and gum disease, oral cancer cases are on the rise in certain regions of Indonesia, often linked to tobacco use and betel nut chewing, particularly in rural areas.
